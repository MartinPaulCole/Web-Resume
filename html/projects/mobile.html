Mobile Radio
Mobile and Handheld Radio Software Maintenance
The products were VHF and UHF, point to point mobile and handheld radios with 12.5KHz and 25KHz channels, FFSK Conventional Conventional Mode CTCSS and Trunked Base-Station Controlled MPT1327 Standard

I was originally employed in the Design Support team performing bug fixing and small enhancememnts on conventional and trunked mobile radios. The platform was Motorola MC68HC11 processors using assembly language. This evolved into a team leadership role managing six software developers both windows and embedded.

Next Generation Mobile Radio Platform
After several years I moved on to a team of ten engineers doing a ground up redevlopment of the aforementioned radio suite. The conventional radio platform was a TIC5402 processor (conventional radios) and a Sparc LEON processor (truned radios). The language used was C. I worked specifically on the driver layer and then on to the UI with a focus on call queuing, tone generation and various other UI tasks.
