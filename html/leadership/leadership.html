Leadership
As I have moved between industries and companies and my experience has increased within these respective environments I have gravitated naturally towards mentoring, planning, advising and, ultimately, leading. Though it is not for everyone, this can often be an inevitability for many during the progression of their careers (as well as other aspects of life in general).

I have very much enjoyed this and learned a lot during it. Feeling passionately about any subject that you have in common with others often results in a desire to help each other learn and grow within it. Being able to take the lead in this process is a privelege that is definitely to be appreciated (though not taken for granted).

My preferences are more by way of technical leadership as opposed to personnel management, however I understand there is a cross-over between these and believe both require similar skills.

Each technology, program, project, team size and team dynamic can be different but I believe there are areas of commonality that can direct leadership style.

The following are thoughts and ideas I have formulated by observing good and bad leaders (as well as being both). Watching how people react to these styles (as well as being aware of my own reactions) has given me an insight into what the traits of good leadership can be.


Good leaders are good followers

Understand differences between individuals

Understand people's motivations

Listen and understand first

Read the room, be available

Adapt your style to the people you manage

Spend the time and sit with people

Let people make mistakes

Learn from other people's experience

If someone does something differently it's ok

Be prepared to be wrong

Knowing when to intervene

Be prepared to state your case but provide evidence

Understand that your experience is valuable but not infallible

Shield your team from outside pressure

Agree mutual goals and ensure they align with the company goals

Set goals that are just a little out of reach

Celebrate success, celebrate success, celebrate success

Plan, plan and plan again

Keep everyone informed at all times

Let go of your own tasks (other people's tasks are more important)
